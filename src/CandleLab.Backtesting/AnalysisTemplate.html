The diagnosis: pyramid mechanics mask the entry edge

This is the differentiated finding and deserves the most careful prose. Explain what a tranche is in the strategy (initial entry + optional pyramid adds after +1R profit). Then show the split below and explain why it's mechanically deterministic: trades that never reach the first-add threshold all lose, trades that do are all lock-in protected and all win. The pyramid doesn't create edge; it reveals whether the entry had any.

Run 1-tranche N 1-tr Win% 1-tr Net 2+ tr N 2+ Win% 2+ Net
Every 1-tranche trade lost; every 2+ tranche trade won. This split is not random, it's structural. The pyramid's lock-in stop guarantees that any trade that triggers the first add closes as a win. The real question the data answers is therefore "what fraction of trades ever reach +1R?" The answer, across all four configurations, is roughly 40-48% — not high enough to overcome the costs and slippage on the other 52-60%.

What this means (and doesn't)

Scope the finding carefully. One year, two instruments, one setup, one timeframe. Resist generalising to "ICT doesn't work" — that's a bar fight you don't want and it's beyond what the evidence supports. What you can say: this specific setup, on these specific instruments, in this specific regime, with realistic costs, does not produce positive expectancy.

Note what you didn't test: longer history, other instruments, other timeframes, stricter filters, different sizing. Invite readers who disagree to run their own variants — the code is open-source. That framing shifts the burden of proof to whoever claims the strategy works.
